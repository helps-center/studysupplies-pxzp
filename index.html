**Must-Have School Supplies**

Here's a breakdown of the stuff you'll probably need for school, from pens to tech.

**1. Pens, Pencils, and All That**

These are the basics for writing stuff down, doing homework, and getting creative.

*   **Pens:**

    *   *Ballpoint:* Your everyday workhorse pen.
    *   *Gel:* Writes smoothly and comes in lots of cool colors, great for when you need to make things stand out.
    *   *Fountain:* A bit fancy, good if you're into nicer handwriting.
    *   *Markers:* Use these for posters and whiteboards.
*   **Pencils:**

    *   *Graphite:* They come in hard and soft and are for sketching or tests.
    *   *Mechanical:* Always sharp, just refill the lead.
    *   *Colored:* For art, diagrams, or making notes colorful.
*   **Other Writing Goodies:**

    *   *Highlighters:* Make important stuff pop in your notes.
    *   *Erasers:* Get rid of pencil marks; white ones are for tiny corrections.
    *   *Sharpeners:* Keep those pencils pointy.

**2. Paper and Notebooks**

The kind of paper you use matters, depending on what you're doing.

*   **Notebooks:** Lined for writing, blank for sketching, graph paper for math.
*   **Loose-leaf:** Put these in binders so you can move pages around.
*   **Sticky notes:** For little reminders, marking pages, or jotting down short notes.
*   **Index cards:** Perfect for making flashcards.
*   **Sketchbooks:** Heavier paper if you like to draw or paint.
*   **Exercise books:** Notebooks that have lines already, these can be for specific stuff, like math.

**3. Get Organized!**

Staying organized makes life way easier.

*   **Folders and Binders:** Keep your schoolwork sorted.
*   **Plastic Sleeves:** Keep important pages from getting wrecked.
*   **Pencil Cases:** Hold your pens, pencils, and other small stuff.
*   **Desk Organizers:** Keep your desk neat and everything within reach.
*   **Backpacks:** Get one that's comfy and has room for everything.

**4. Tools for Math and Drawing**

If you're in math, science, or art, you'll need these.

*   **Rulers:** Get one that's see-through for accurate drawing.
*   **Protractors:** For measuring angles in geometry.
*   **Compasses:** For making circles.
*   **Set Squares:** Triangles will help with doing technical drawings.
*   **Measuring Tape:** For bigger projects.

**5. Art Supplies**

For when you're feeling creative.

*   **Paints:** Watercolors, acrylics, whatever you like.
*   **Brushes:** Get a few different sizes.
*   **Pastels and Crayons:** Oil pastels, soft pastels, wax crayons.
*   **Glue:** Stick glue, the liquid stuff, whatever gets the job done.
*   **Scissors:** Regular ones and maybe some smaller ones for crafts.
*   **Craft Paper:** Construction paper, colored paper, poster board – stock up!

**6. Tech Tools**

School's gone digital, so here's what you might need.

*   **Calculators:** Get one that does what you need, from basic math to graphing.
*   **Tablets/Laptops:** For doing research, typing up papers, and using learning apps.
*   **USB Drives:** Back up your files!
*   **Headphones/Earbuds:** Block out noise and pay attention in online classes.
*   **Digital Stylus:** For drawing or writing on your tablet.

**7. Study Helpers**

Stuff that makes studying easier.

*   **Whiteboards:** Great for brainstorming or just visualizing stuff.
*   **Planners:** Write down when stuff is due.
*   **Bookmarks:** Mark your spot in books.
*   **Staplers:** Keep papers together.
*   **Tape:** For fixing mistakes.

**8. Keep It Safe!**

Take care of your stuff.

*   **Book Covers:** Keep your books from getting beat up.
*   **Desk Mats:** So you have a smooth place to write.
*   **Cleaning Wipes:** Keep your stuff clean.

**9. Make Your Study Space Nice**

Make sure you have everything you need to learn well.

*   **Good Lighting:** You don't want to strain your eyes.
*   **Comfy Chair:** Helps you pay attention.
*   **Quiet:** Find a place where you will not be bothered.

**Some Tips:**

*   **Go for quality:** It'll last longer.
*   **Sort stuff:** Keep stuff from different classes separate.
*   **Make it yours:** Use colors and labels to tell everything apart.
*   **Buy early:** Don't wait until the last minute!
*   **Check what's required:** Some schools have rules about what you need to bring.
